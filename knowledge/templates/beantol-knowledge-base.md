Roast Philosophy

We roast each bean according to its origin and profile — not one-size-fits-all.

• Espresso roasts: Mostly medium-dark, for espresso extraction and milk-based drinks.
• Filter / pour-over roasts: Mostly lighter, for fruity, origin-forward cups.
• Roast levels in general: Light preserves fruit and acidity; medium balances body and sweetness; medium-dark (our espresso default) suits espresso and milk with chocolatey/nutty notes; very dark roasts are heavier and more bitter — not our default.
• Freshness: Roast date is printed on the back of each pouch. We roast in small batches on demand.


Brewing & Grind

Grind size by method:
• Espresso: fine
• Pour-over: medium-fine to medium
• Drip: medium
• French press: coarse

General tips: Water around 90–96°C (just off boil) for most methods. Espresso extraction often around 25–30 seconds as a starting guide. Match advice to the customer’s bean and equipment when known.

We typically sell whole beans. Grind depends on their equipment. Grinding at purchase can sometimes be arranged — ask in chat or at the shop.

Storage & Freshness

• Roast date on the back of the pouch.
• Best flavor within 2–4 weeks of roast; still good for several weeks if stored well; stale after ~2–3 months opened.
• Store airtight, cool and dry, away from sunlight, heat, and strong odors.
• Do not refrigerate (moisture and odors).
• Freezing whole beans is optional for long storage only if truly airtight — thaw once and use; most home users should buy fresh amounts and store at room temperature in a sealed bag.
